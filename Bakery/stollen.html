Stollen

All Bakery Recipes

Ingredients

Switch to Steps
  • 6 oz Dried Mixed Fruit
  • 2 tsp Rum
  • 3 cups Strong Plain Flour
  • pinch of Salt
  • 7 gr PAcket of Fast Action Dried Yeast
  • 6 Tbsp Butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup (150ml) Milk, warm
  • 1 Egg
  • 6 oz White Marzipan
  • 1 Tbsp Butter, melted
  • Powdered Sugar

Steps

Switch to Ingredients
  1. Tip the dried fruit into a small bowl and add the lemon rind and rum.
    Stir the fruit, then cover the bowl.
    Leave the fruit for at least 2 hrs or overnight.

  2. To make the dough, tip the flour into a bowl and stir in the salt and yeast.

  3. Lightly beat together the butter, milk and egg and add the flour mixture.
    Mix together to form a soft dough.

  4. Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead it for 8-10 minutes,
    until the dough is smooth and elastic.

  5. Make a dip in the center of the dough and pour in the fruit and any juices remaining in the bowl.

  6. Knead the fruite into the dough.

  7. Roll the dough out to about 10"x8".

  8. Knead the marzipan to soften it and then roll it into a long sausage shape, just slightly shorter then the dough.

  9. Place the marzipan on the dough and then fold the dough over it.
    Bring the other side of the dough over into the center, to give it a slighly oval shape.

  10. Place the stollen on a buttered baking sheet, and cover it with a light oiled piece of cling film.

  11. Leave in a warm place until stollen has doubled in size.

  12. Set the oven to 400F.

  13. Bake the stollen, towards the top of the oven, for 20-30 minutes,
    or until it sounds hollow when lifted off the baking sheet and tapped underneath.

  14. Remove the stollen from the oven and slide onto a wire rack.
    Immediately brush over the melted butter and heavily dredge it with powdered sugar.

  15. Leave it to cool. The stollen will keep for up to 5-7 werks
    if wrapped tighly in a foil and stored in a cool place.
    Redust with powdered sugar before serving.
